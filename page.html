Introduction

Objective: To promote safer road designs and systems that protect animals and prevent accidents.

Problem Statement

Define the Issue

In the Philippines, roadkill and hit-and-run incidents involving stray animals, particularly dogs and cats, are common but poorly documented in official statistics. Rapid urbanization and high vehicle density exacerbate the problem.

Estimated Number of Strays

According to the Department of Agricultureâ€™s Bureau of Animal Industry (BAI), there are an estimated 12 million stray dogs and cats in the Philippines as of 2019.

Animal Rescue Reports

Animal welfare organizations, such as PAWS and CARA, report dealing with hundreds of cases of injured or killed animals yearly in Metro Manila.

Road Accidents Involving Strays

Studies by the MMDA indicate that stray animals are often involved in road accidents, contributing to traffic incidents, especially at night.

Hit-and-Run Laws

The Anti-Rabies Act of 2007 imposes fines for letting dogs roam freely but lacks specific laws for drivers involved in hit-and-run incidents with stray animals.

Proposed Solutions

  • Incorporate animal crossings like overpasses and underpasses.
  • Use smart road technology for early detection of animals.
  • Create designated "stray zones" with speed reduction and barriers.
  • Develop data-driven infrastructure modifications.
  • Implement community-based stray management programs.

Conclusion

As future civil engineers, we must advocate for road designs that ensure safety for both humans and animals. By implementing innovative solutions and fostering compassion, we can create a harmonious coexistence between urban development and animal welfare.
